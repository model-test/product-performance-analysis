# Product Performance Analysis & Return Impact Investigation
Interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing product performance, profitability, regional trends, and return behavior through business intelligence reporting.

## Project Overview

This project analyzes product performance, profitability, regional trends, and return behavior using Power BI. The goal was not only to visualize business performance but also to investigate areas that may deserve further attention and identify opportunities for improvement.

The dashboard was designed as an interactive business intelligence solution that allows users to explore revenue, profit, profit margins, production costs, regional performance, category performance, and return activity through filters, drilldowns, and dynamic visualizations.

## Project Objectives

The purpose of this project was to explore:

* How products perform financially.
* Which categories generate the greatest profit.
* Which products and regions contribute most to returns.
* How return behavior changes over time.
* Where future business investigations should be focused.

The project was approached as a business analysis exercise rather than simply a visualization exercise. The focus was not only on identifying patterns, but also on understanding why those patterns may matter and what actions should be considered next.

---

# Executive Summary

The analysis found that the company maintains strong overall profitability and a relatively low return rate. However, several areas were identified that may warrant further investigation due to their potential financial impact.

---

## Key Finding 1: Overall Return Rate Remains Relatively Low

### Observation

The overall return rate across all orders was **5.5%**.

### Interpretation

The company does not currently appear to have a widespread return problem. Most products are being retained by customers, suggesting that overall product satisfaction and operational performance are generally healthy.

### Recommendation

Continue monitoring customer feedback, product quality metrics, and return trends to maintain the relatively low return rate and identify emerging issues before they become larger operational concerns.

---

## Key Finding 2: Southwest Exhibits the Highest Return Rate

### Observation

The Southwest region generated the highest return rate among all regions.

### Interpretation

Customers within the Southwest may be experiencing a different product experience than customers in other regions. While the data cannot identify the exact cause, this region represents the greatest opportunity for reducing return-related losses.

### Recommendation

Investigate customer reviews, return behavior, operational processes, and product quality concerns within the Southwest region. Determine whether regional factors are contributing to elevated return activity compared to other locations.

---

## Key Finding 3: December Experiences the Largest Return Spike

### Observation

December generated the highest gross sales returns and exhibited a return rate of approximately **10.45%**, significantly higher than the overall company return rate.

### Interpretation

A disproportionate amount of annual return activity occurs during December. This suggests that seasonal conditions may be influencing customer behavior, operational performance, or product outcomes during this period.

### Recommendation

Investigate whether seasonal factors contribute to elevated return activity during December. One possible explanation is that seasonal weather conditions may negatively affect shipping conditions, which could impact product condition upon arrival and ultimately customer satisfaction.

Customer reviews should also be examined to determine whether a gap exists between customer expectations and the actual product experience during this period.

The objective is not to assume that shipping conditions are the cause, but to determine whether a relationship exists between seasonal conditions, customer satisfaction, and elevated return behavior.

---

## Key Finding 4: Computers Generate the Largest Gross Sales Returns

### Observation

The Computers category generated the highest gross sales returns among all product categories.

### Interpretation

Computer-related returns create the greatest financial impact on the business. The financial impact may be driven by high product prices, high sales volume, elevated return activity, or a combination of these factors.

### Recommendation

Perform additional analysis to determine whether the financial impact is primarily caused by pricing, sales volume, or product-specific quality concerns. Understanding the primary driver will help prioritize future improvement efforts.

---

## Key Finding 5: Laptops Generate Significant Return Revenue Despite a Relatively Low Return Rate

### Observation

Laptops generated substantial return-related revenue despite maintaining a relatively low return rate compared to some other products.

### Interpretation

High-value products can create significant financial losses even when return frequency remains relatively low. This demonstrates that financial impact and return rate should not be evaluated independently.

### Recommendation

Compare laptop performance against other high-value products to determine whether pricing, customer expectations, product quality, or other factors are responsible for the observed financial impact.

---

# Overall Conclusion

The company appears financially healthy overall, with strong profitability and a relatively low return rate.

However, several opportunities for further investigation were identified:

* Southwest return behavior.
* December return activity.
* Computer category return losses.
* Laptop return-related financial impact.

While the current dataset cannot establish definitive root causes, these areas represent the most promising opportunities for future analysis and potential operational improvement.

# Limitations

This analysis was performed using a limited dataset and several important business variables were unavailable.

The dataset does not include:

* Customer review data
* Return reason codes
* Shipping performance metrics
* Customer support interactions
* Inventory information
* Multi-year historical data

As a result, the analysis can identify patterns and opportunities for investigation but cannot definitively determine root causes.

---

# Future Improvements

Potential future enhancements include:

# How to Use

1. Open the `.pbix` file in Power BI Desktop.
2. Use the Product slicer to filter dashboard results.
3. Navigate between the Product Performance Analysis page and the Analysis on Returns page.
4. Interact with visuals to explore category, product, and regional performance.
5. Use drilldowns where available to investigate trends at a deeper level.
